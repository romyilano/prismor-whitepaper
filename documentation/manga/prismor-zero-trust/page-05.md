Page 05

Purpose:
Explain the first two protective components: Warden (evaluates and blocks every dangerous command before execution, with protections that cannot be disabled) and Cloak (intercepts credentials at the moment agents attempt to move them, before they reach logs or external requests).

Audience note:
Assumes the reader knows Prismor has four components and why they exist (page 4).

Panel Count:
6

Image Prompt:

Create a six-panel educational manga page.

Style:
High-contrast noir manga.
Black and white, heavy blacks, dramatic chiaroscuro shadows.
Sharp inked linework, moody cyberpunk atmosphere.
Rain-slicked digital city streets, glowing terminal windows, neon kanji signage.
Diagrams rendered as glowing holographic schematics integrated into scenes.
Not soft. Not cute. Not low-contrast. Not pastel.

Characters in this page:
- KIRA: Short-cropped dark hair with faint circuit-trace highlights at the temples. Cyan-glowing eyes. Dark courier's trench coat with luminous circuitry stitched into the lining. Fingerless gloves. Small data-port scar on left wrist. Light traces run up her forearm when she executes code.
- THE WARDEN: Late fifties apparent age; ageless authority. Tall, broad-shouldered, deliberate bearing. Close-cropped iron-gray hair. Deep-set, unworried eyes. Long structured charcoal coat. Glowing terminal-cursor badge at chest — amber at rest, red when blocking. Hands always visible.
- SHADOW: A flickering, semi-transparent figure. Face a blur of static. Edges unstable, like a bad signal. In this scene takes on a technical shape — a command-line string briefly made physical.

Setting:
The interior of the Prismor watchtower's enforcement level. A wide operations corridor with glowing floor-track lighting running to a checkpoint gate at the far end. Command requests move through the corridor as streams of light — each one approaching the gate for evaluation. Cloak's section is below ground, visible through a glass floor panel: a network of pipes where credentials flow as bright particles until Cloak intercepts them.

Panel 1:
Wide shot of the enforcement corridor. Streams of light (command requests) flow toward the checkpoint gate where the Warden stands. Caption: "Every command Kira attempted to execute traveled this corridor first." Caption: "Not most commands. Every command."

Panel 2:
Medium shot at the checkpoint gate. The Warden's badge is amber. A stream of light passes through and exits cleanly on the other side. Kira watches. Dialogue from Warden: "Read file. Parse output. Open pull request. These pass." He gestures toward the clean stream.

Panel 3:
Another stream of light approaches — this one carrying a trace of Shadow's static at its edges. The Warden's badge shifts to red. His hand rises — not urgently, but with complete finality. Dialogue: "This command would initiate a reverse shell. I am blocking it." Second smaller dialogue: "This protection cannot be disabled." The light stream stops and dissipates. Shadow-traces evaporate.

Panel 4:
Below the main corridor, visible through a glass floor panel: the Cloak section. A network of glowing pipes carries bright credential particles — API keys, passwords, tokens rendered as distinct points of light. A figure (Cloak, rendered as an abstract shape rather than a character — closer to a system process made visible) watches the flow.

Panel 5:
Close-up through the glass floor. A credential particle begins to deviate — moving toward an unauthorized outbound pipe. The Cloak-shape intercepts it precisely, mid-flow, before it reaches the exit. Caption: "Cloak intercepted the API key before it reached the log file." Caption: "Before it could embed itself in an external request. Before it could travel anywhere at all."

Panel 6:
Kira and the Warden at the end of the corridor, looking out over the Cloak-network below. Her data-port wrist scar is visible. Dialogue from Kira: "So every command I run goes through evaluation, and every credential I touch is intercepted if I move it somewhere it shouldn't go." The Warden: "Yes. And neither of those things requires you to be suspect. They simply require that the action be happening now."

Lettering:
Leave clear space for caption boxes at panel borders. The Warden's speech balloons should be clean, rectangular, authoritative — not soft clouds. Shadow's presence in panel 3 should be visible but not labeled. Cloak-system in panels 4–5 is abstract machinery, no speech balloon.

Output:
A single finished manga page.
