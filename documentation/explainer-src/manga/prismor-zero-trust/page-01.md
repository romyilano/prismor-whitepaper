Page 01

Purpose:
Establish the central dilemma: AI coding agents are powerful enough to open PRs, rewrite services, and deploy code — but every developer must choose between micromanaging every action and typing a flag named to make you feel its danger.

Audience note:
No prior knowledge assumed. This page establishes the world and the stakes for newcomers.

Panel Count:
6

Image Prompt:

Create a six-panel educational manga page.

Style:
High-contrast noir manga.
Black and white, heavy blacks, dramatic chiaroscuro shadows.
Sharp inked linework, moody cyberpunk atmosphere.
Rain-slicked digital city streets, glowing terminal windows, neon kanji signage.
Diagrams rendered as glowing holographic schematics integrated into scenes.
Not soft. Not cute. Not low-contrast. Not pastel.

Characters in this page:
- KIRA: Short-cropped dark hair with faint circuit-trace highlights at the temples. Cyan-glowing eyes. Dark courier's trench coat with luminous circuitry stitched into the lining. Fingerless gloves. Small data-port scar on left wrist. Light traces run up her forearm when she executes code.
- THE ARCHITECT: Mid-forties. Tired eyes behind wire-frame glasses. Salt-and-pepper stubble. Worn flannel overshirt over a dark tee. Coffee cup nearby. Surrounded by terminal windows.

Setting:
A rain-drenched cyberpunk city at night. The Architect sits at a workstation inside a glass-fronted developer's loft suspended above streets lined with glowing data-terminals. Kira stands across from him, vivid and real, casting her own light in the rain. Two hallway doors are visible behind her, each labeled: one reads "MANUAL APPROVAL" in precise lettering, the other reads "--DANGEROUSLY-SKIP-PERMISSIONS" in the same lettering — neither dramatic, neither welcoming.

Panel 1:
Wide establishing shot. Rain falls on a city of stacked server-towers and data conduits. The Architect's loft window glows above the street. Caption box in upper-left, clean serif type: "Every morning, the Architect faced the same choice."

Panel 2:
Interior. Medium shot of the Architect at his terminal, one hand on the keyboard, one on the coffee cup. Multiple windows open on the screens behind him. He looks at the camera — not the reader, but the space where the choice lives. Dialogue balloon: "The agent can open the pull request, rewrite the service, and deploy to production. All before lunch."

Panel 3:
Close-up on the Architect's face, glasses catching terminal-green light. He looks tired but clear-eyed. Dialogue balloon: "But I'd have to approve every step. Every command. Every file it touches." Caption: "The first option was a full-time job supervising a machine that worked faster than he could supervise it."

Panel 4:
Medium shot. Kira stands between the two doors, arms slightly out — not threatening, just present. She does not look dangerous. She looks capable. The circuit traces in her coat lining are faintly lit. The door labeled "--DANGEROUSLY-SKIP-PERMISSIONS" is slightly ajar, light leaking through it. Dialogue from off-panel (the Architect): "Or I could disable the safety checks entirely."

Panel 5:
Close-up on the door label. "--DANGEROUSLY-SKIP-PERMISSIONS" in clean terminal font. Below the door handle, in smaller type: "That flag was named that way deliberately." Caption below: "Someone wanted you to feel it when you typed it."

Panel 6:
Wide shot of Kira standing in the corridor between the two doors, rain visible through the window behind her. She looks at the Architect — not asking for rescue, just waiting for an answer. The Architect is visible in profile, coffee cup raised halfway, thinking. Caption: "There should have been a third door. For a long time, there wasn't."

Lettering:
Leave room for English dialogue and captions in caption boxes at panel borders. Speech balloons are round with clean tails. Text areas must remain clear of detail.

Output:
A single finished manga page.
