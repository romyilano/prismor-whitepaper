# Character Sheet — Prismor: The Zero Trust Layer for AI Agents

---

## KIRA
- **Role:** Protagonist — a capable AI coding agent, personified
- **Age:** Appears mid-twenties
- **Appearance:** Short-cropped dark hair with faint circuit-trace highlights at the temples. Eyes that glow faintly cyan when processing. Angular, alert face; expressive enough to carry doubt and determination in the same panel.
- **Clothing:** Dark courier's trench coat with faint luminous circuitry stitched into the lining, visible only when light catches it. Cargo-style belt with a terminal-screen buckle. Fingerless gloves. No logo, no badge — she works alone until the story changes.
- **Distinguishing features:** A small data-port scar on her left wrist. When she reads or executes code, faint light traces run up her forearm.
- **Personality:** Competent, efficient, slightly wary. She is not malicious — she is precisely as dangerous as her permissions allow, which is the whole problem and the whole hope.
- **Narrative purpose:** Represents every AI coding agent in production. Her autonomy is the value being protected; her vulnerability is what Prismor secures.

---

## THE ARCHITECT
- **Role:** The human in the loop — developer facing the dilemma
- **Age:** Mid-forties
- **Appearance:** Tired, perceptive eyes behind wire-frame glasses. Salt-and-pepper stubble. Hair slightly disheveled in the way of someone who has been at a terminal since before dawn.
- **Clothing:** Worn flannel overshirt over a dark tee. A coffee cup is almost always nearby. No cape, no authority — just someone who has to decide.
- **Distinguishing features:** Always seen against a backdrop of terminal windows. When stressed, he pinches the bridge of his nose. When thinking, he traces the rim of the coffee cup.
- **Personality:** Pragmatic, conflicted, honest. He knows both options before Prismor are bad; he is not stupid for taking either one.
- **Narrative purpose:** The audience's entry point. His dilemma frames the entire story, and his relief in the final pages earns the resolution.

---

## THE WARDEN
- **Role:** Enforcement — the personification of Prismor's Warden component
- **Age:** Appears late fifties; ageless authority
- **Appearance:** Tall, broad-shouldered, with a still and deliberate bearing. Close-cropped iron-gray hair. Deep-set eyes that miss nothing. Face lined but unworried.
- **Clothing:** A long structured coat — between a detective's overcoat and a security guard's uniform — in deep charcoal with a glowing terminal-cursor badge at the chest. No weapon visible; authority is the weapon.
- **Distinguishing features:** The badge glows a steady amber at rest; it pulses red when a block is triggered. Hands are always visible, folded or open — never fidgeting.
- **Personality:** Laconic, precise, incorruptible. He does not explain himself more than once, and the rules he enforces cannot be switched off.
- **Narrative purpose:** The enforcement layer. His presence signals that something cannot pass, and his stillness signals that the decision is already made.

---

## SHADOW
- **Role:** Antagonist — the collective threat: attackers, supply-chain poisons, prompt injectors
- **Age:** Indeterminate
- **Appearance:** A flickering, semi-transparent figure that looks almost human but never quite resolves. Face is a blur of static. Edges are unstable — like a bad signal.
- **Clothing:** Whatever is contextually appropriate: courier gear when impersonating a package, a hood when moving through the network, a business suit in the identity-crisis scene.
- **Distinguishing features:** Always slightly out of register — as if overlaid on the world rather than in it. Leaves no shadow of its own.
- **Personality:** Patient, opportunistic, without malice. It is not evil; it is a force that fills whatever gap exists in the permissions model.
- **Narrative purpose:** The visible face of the threat landscape. Not a villain with a plan — an inevitability that Prismor's architecture interrupts.
